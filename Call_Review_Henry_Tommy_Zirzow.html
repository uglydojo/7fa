13-PHASE BREAKDOWN

1
INTRODUCTION + RAPPORT
Framework: 2 min. Geographic icebreaker, find connection, use humor.
Henry opened light. "Glad to be in the call with you. Heard some good things." Tommy complimented his glasses. Natural, easy, no forced energy.
2
FRAMING + EXPECTATIONS
Framework: Set the rules. Get agreement to be transparent.
"This is usually about 30, max 40 minutes. I'll ask questions. If it makes sense, next steps. If not, you leave with value." Clean frame.
3
DISCOVERY / WHY CHAIN
Framework: Keep asking WHY until you hit emotion. Go 5-6 levels deep.
Asked "what would 40-50K change?" Got Level 1 (hiring). Asked "personally?" Tommy went to Level 2-3: income, family, freedom, 25 years old, betting everything, wants a family. Then Henry said "thank you for being transparent" and moved on. Never reached Level 4-5.
Should have asked: "What happens if TMS+ doesn't grow?" / "How does staying at $17K/month affect the family timeline you mentioned?" / "You've been talking about niching for 2 years. What's kept it from happening?"
4
CLOSE DOORS
Framework: Pre-handle objections: Decision maker? Burned before? Budget ready? Timeline?
Tommy handed Henry three open doors. Dad "is not a believer in coaching." Tommy is only "part of" the decision maker. Tommy later asked how to justify $18K/year vs other investments. Henry closed zero doors.
Tommy: "My dad is not a believer in it. The whole coaching stuff."
Henry: "Gotcha. Okay."
Should have asked: "Should we get Bill on the call Friday?" / "What would your dad need to see to feel good about this?" / "If this is a fit, are you in a position to move forward today?"
5
TIE DOWNS
Framework: Use THEIR words. Make pain concrete. "Earlier you said..." Hold them accountable.
Tommy gave a goldmine of tie-down material: $319/month retainers, no SOPs, no follow-up sequence, no niche after 2 years, "behind the eight ball," "sales process is all over the place," pricing is "insanely low." During the 11-minute pitch, Henry never once used any of Tommy's words. Every feature was presented generically.
Should have said: "You told me your sales process is all over the place. This is the exact SOP that fixes that." / "You said you have no follow-up sequence and you're behind the eight ball. This system automates that entirely."
6
PRESENT THE OFFER
Framework: 3-5 min. Match THEIR pain to the solution. Show how it fixes THEIR specific problems. NO PRICE YET.
Henry ran an 11-minute uninterrupted feature monologue (minutes 35-46). Listed the portal, coaching, SOPs, lead gen, cold outreach, webinars, LinkedIn, ads, white-label vendors, hiring, onboarding, events. None of it was mapped to Tommy's specific problems. Tommy's only responses during this stretch: "yep," "nope," "there was a lot there."
Tommy: "Yeah. Yep. There was a lot there, but okay."
7
TEMP CHECK #1 (PRE-PRICE)
Framework: "On a scale of 1-10, where are you?" Separate money from solution if needed.
Never happened. Henry went straight from the feature dump to "any questions?" to price. He had no idea if Tommy was at a 4 or a 9 before introducing the investment.
8
STACK THE OFFER (LOOP)
Framework: "Does that make sense?" Question -> Answer -> RESTACK. Repeat 5-6x if needed.
Henry's own technique from the framework: "I've done this 5-6 times on one call." He never stacked once on this call. He dumped features in a monologue and asked "any questions?" at the end. That's not stacking.
9
FORECAST THE FUTURE
Framework: Paint what happens AFTER they buy. Onboarding, first call, 90-day plan. Make it real.
Never happened. Should have painted: "Tommy, imagine 90 days from now. Home services niche locked in. Pricing at $1,500 minimum. SOPs built. Follow-up system running automatically. Same 40 clients = $60K/month instead of $17K. Your dad sees those numbers and says this was the right call."
10
DROP THE PRICE + SHUT UP
Framework: "The investment is $X per month." Then SILENCE. Even if 2 minutes. Hold the tension.
Henry stated "$1,497 month by month" but immediately kept talking: 90-day guarantee, no contracts, month-to-month, easy cancellation, pause anytime. He filled the silence instead of holding it.
11
TEMP CHECK #2 (POST-PRICE)
Framework: "Now that you know everything and the investment, scale of 1-10, does this solve your problem?"
Never asked. Went straight from price to "I understand. When can you get back to me?" No chance to surface objections. No chance to handle them. No idea where Tommy stood.
12
ASSUMPTIVE CLOSE
Framework: "Most people pay with Visa, MasterCard, or American Express. Which one?"
Henry never attempted to close. Not once. No assumptive close. No "should we get you started?" No "are you ready to move forward?" He accepted "I'll need to review" without any attempt.
13
ADT FRAMEWORK
Framework: Agree -> Duplicate -> Transition with question. Set 3-day follow-up if needed.
Tommy said "I hate to pause it, not give you an answer now. Give me a week." Henry said "yeah" and booked a week-out follow-up. Framework says 3 days, not a week.
Tommy: "I hate to pause it, not give you an answer now."

WHAT HAPPENED vs WHAT SHOULD HAPPEN

❌ The Call

  • 🗣️ 11-minute uninterrupted feature monologue (min 35-46). Tommy's only responses: "yep," "nope," "there was a lot there"
  • 🔇 Never tied a single feature to Tommy's specific pain points
  • 🚪 Tommy said dad is "not a believer in coaching." Henry said "okay" and moved on
  • ⏳ Accepted "give me a week" without resistance. Framework says 3 days max
  • ❌ Never attempted to close. Not once. Zero close attempts on a 56-minute call

✅ Framework Says

  • 🔗 "You told me you have no follow-up sequence. This is the system that fixes that."
  • 🔍 "You've had 2 years to pick a niche and haven't. What's been the blocker?"
  • 👨‍👦 "Should we get Bill on the call Friday so I can answer his questions directly?"
  • 🎯 ADT when Tommy asks for a week: "What specifically needs to happen in that week?"
  • 💳 Temp check before price. Temp check after price. Then: "Visa, MasterCard, or AmEx?"

THE FATAL MISTAKE

Henry let Tommy off the hook by accepting "maybe" and "give me a week" without challenge.

The right question: "What's been stopping you from fixing the pricing, building SOPs, and picking a niche for the past two years on your own?"

Forces Tommy to confront that he CAN'T do it alone. He's had 2 years to niche and hasn't. 2 years to raise prices and hasn't. 2 years to build SOPs and hasn't. The answer writes itself.

TOP 3 COACHING PRIORITIES

1

Stop Pushing the Boulder

The 11-minute feature monologue is the #1 problem. Cut it. Replace with questions. "You told me you have no SOPs, no follow-up sequence, and you've been trying to niche for 2 years. What's been the blocker?" Let Tommy tell you why he needs this. When THEY push, they're invested. When YOU push, they watch.

2

Use Both Temp Checks

Before price: "On a scale of 1-10, putting price aside, does this solve the SOPs, pricing, and niche problem?" After price: "Now that you know it's $1,497/month, where are you?" These two questions surface every objection and give you a chance to handle them. You skipped both. That means you went into price blind and left with no way to recover.

3

Never Accept "Give Me a Week" Without ADT

Agree: "I'm with you, big decision." Duplicate: "Sounds like you want to run this by your dad first." Transition: "Is there anything ELSE, other than that conversation, stopping you?" Find the real objection. Address it before they leave. And set a 3-day follow-up, not a week. More time = more reasons to say no.
