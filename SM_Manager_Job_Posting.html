About the Role

The Marketing Manager is responsible for strategy, content creation, and execution of all social media activities across Seven Figure Agency's platforms. This is a leadership-level role requiring independent decision-making, brand voice understanding, and the ability to manage real-time content during live events. You'll report to the COO and work closely with the Head of Sales & Business Development.

This is NOT a coordinator role. The Marketing Manager creates strategy and content, not just executes tasks given to them. You'll own the social presence and drive results.

What You'll Do

Content Strategy & Planning

  • Develop weekly/monthly content calendars
  • Plan platform-specific content (Instagram, Facebook, LinkedIn, YouTube)
  • Align content with marketing campaigns, intensives, and launches
  • Identify content opportunities from member wins, testimonials, sessions

Content Creation

  • Write all social media posts (captions, hooks, CTAs)
  • Create platform-appropriate variations (carousel for IG, text-heavy for FB, professional for LinkedIn)
  • Select photos and graphics for posts
  • Write captions for short-form video content (Reels, TikTok, Shorts)

Brand Voice & Tone

  • Maintain Josh's voice and tone across all platforms
  • Maintain Yesenia's voice for her personal profiles
  • Understand and apply the 7FA brand guidelines
  • Present speakers and members as "heroes" in content

Intensive & Event Coverage

  • Post award winner announcements

Test Project 2: Content Calendar Exercise

This test evaluates your ability to think strategically about content planning, platform-specific execution, and audience targeting.

You will be evaluated on:
  • Platform-specific thinking (what works on LinkedIn vs Instagram vs Stories)
  • Posting cadence (not just what, but when)
  • Audience awareness (members vs prospects)
  • FOMO and urgency tactics
  • Creativity and brand voice alignment

The Scenario

You're the Marketing Manager at Seven Figure Agency. Below is a list of content themes and events happening in one week. Your job is to create a content calendar that maps out what to post, when, and on which platform.

Week of February 10-14, 2026

Day Event/Theme
Monday New member spotlight (Devon Clark joined, runs a home services agency at $25K MRR)
Tuesday Josh's Tuesday Training drops (topic: "How to Run a 15-Minute Sales Call")
Wednesday Testimonial video from existing member (Forrest Schwartz, grew from $40K to $80K MRR)
Thursday March Intensive early bird deadline reminder (event is March 2-3 in Miami)
Friday Weekend engagement post (community-focused)

Platforms to Cover:

  • Instagram (Feed + Stories + Reels)
  • Facebook (Page + Group)
  • YouTube (Shorts + Community tab)
  • LinkedIn

Your Deliverable

Create a content calendar in table or list format. For each post, include:

  • Day and time to post
  • Platform (be specific: IG Feed vs IG Stories vs IG Reels)
  • Content type (photo, carousel, video, text post, story)
  • Hook or caption concept (first line or headline)
  • Call to action (what do you want them to do?)

Example Entry:

Monday 10am | IG Feed | Photo + caption

Hook: "New member alert. Meet Devon Clark..."

CTA: "Drop a welcome in the comments"

Important Notes:

  • Plan multiple posts per day — Each theme can be posted across several platforms, not just once. Think about how the same content gets adapted for different channels.
  • Audience matters: Facebook Group = existing members only. Facebook Page, LinkedIn, Instagram = prospects can see these. Tailor your messaging accordingly.
  • Use platform features: Stories have polls, countdowns, and question stickers. Reels need hooks in the first 2 seconds. LinkedIn favors text-heavy or carousel posts. Show us you know the differences.
  • Be specific with timing: Don't just say "morning." Tell us 9am, 10am, etc. and why that time makes sense for that platform.
